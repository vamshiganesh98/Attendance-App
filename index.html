What this app does

This app helps you take daily class attendance and keep a running register — without any paperwork. Open the app, tap each student who is present, and save. Everyone you don't tap is marked absent automatically.

Every time you save, the attendance is written straight into a Google Sheet in the same register format you're used to — students as rows, dates as columns, P for present and A for absent, colour-coded in green and red.

The three tabs

  1. Attendance — mark today's class. Tap a student card to mark present, tap again for absent. "All Present" and "All Absent" speed things up on easy days.
  2. Records — see overall attendance %, per-student stats, and a day-by-day history table.
  3. Settings — connect to Google Sheets, manage the student list, and run a one-time import of old records.

How data flows

The app talks to a small Google Apps Script that lives inside your Google account. When you save attendance, the app sends the data to that script, which writes it into your spreadsheet. When you open the app or tap sync, it reads the spreadsheet back so the Records tab stays up to date — useful if attendance was also updated from another device.

Works everywhere

Because everything is stored in Google Sheets, the same data is visible whether you open this app on a phone, tablet, or laptop. Add it to your iPhone home screen for a native app feel — open Safari, tap Share, then "Add to Home Screen".

Need to start a new register, say for a new term or batch? Just create a fresh Google Sheet, paste its link in Settings under "Spreadsheet", and the app instantly switches to it.
